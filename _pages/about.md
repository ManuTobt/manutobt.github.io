My name is Manuel Blau, a climate and cryosphere scientist working at Seoul National University with [Jong-Seong Kug](https://climate.snu.ac.kr/people-jong-seong-kug/). I am working on the response of mountain climate to geoengineering and climate mitigation approaches. In particular, we assume sulfite aerosol injection into the stratosphere. I received my Ph.D. from the [Department of Climate System](https://his.pusan.ac.kr/sites/climate/index.do) at the Pusan National University and the [Institute for Basic Sciences Center for Climate Physics](https://ibsclimate.org/) under the supervision of [Kyung-Ja Ha](https://ibsclimate.org/people/%ed%95%98%ea%b2%bd%ec%9e%90-kyung-ja-ha/).

My research interests are centered on mountain and polar climate, climate dynamics, and teleconnection of climate variability. I have experiance in mass-balance modeling of the Greenland outlet glaciers, and the cryosphere/surface - atmosphere interactions. A special interest was the response of cryosphere (glaciers and snow cover) to climate change and trends, as well as to climate variability. Other interests are the surface-atmosphere dynamics in lights of the energy imbalance and the associated climate dynamics and variability. Further, I have increasing interest in the teleconnection of tropical variability and climate trends on the hydroclimate, droughts, and temperature extremes in Africa and Southeast Asia.

In near future, I will start a new position at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology in the group of [Yu Liang](https://facultyprofiles.hkust-gz.edu.cn/). We will set a new focus on heatwave and heat stress in South East Asia.
